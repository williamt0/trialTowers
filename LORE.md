# THE TOWER — Lore Bible

> *The Tower was not built. It grew.*

## The Core Myth

The first stone was **found**, not laid — and the finding woke it. Since that day the Tower has
grown **one floor for every age of the world**, swallowing realms whole as it rises. It does not
destroy what it takes. **It keeps.** A meadow, a jungle, an empire — each floor is a world the
Tower decided to remember, complete with its people, who still farm and trade and light their
lamps inside it. Life insists.

At the top sits **the Crown — the Empty Throne**. It is not a prize. It is a hunger wearing a
chair's shape. The Tower offers every conqueror a seat:

- **Sit**, and you feed the Tower for an age. You become its heart, its warmth, its reason.
- **Refuse**, and the Tower — slighted — sharpens every floor and grows the climb crueler to break
  you. *(This is New Game+. The Tower takes rejection personally.)*

The throne has been refused exactly **once**. The Tower built the tenth floor that same night.

## The Gatekeepers

Every gatekeeper **was a climber once**. Few conquerors have the stomach for the Crown, so the
Tower offers lesser seats — a floor, a stair, a duty. Most accept. The thing you fight before
every stair is what acceptance looks like after a hundred years. *Ask any gatekeeper what they
were before. Watch the answer hurt them.*

## The Mechanisms (in-world explanations)

- **Waystones** are the first climbers, who gave their bones to the road. Press your ear close — they still answer. (They *SHOUT*, the hermits say.)
- **Memory orbs** are the Tower thinking out loud. Touch one and it thinks AT you — these grant the **Twelve Echoes**, the Tower's own story, remembered forever across all your heroes.
- **The Floor Warden** is not a beast. It is the Tower's hand, sent for climbers who linger like splinters.
- **The Watch, the hamlets, the markets** on the lower floors: the swallowed peoples, living on. The Tower keeps what it takes — including their laws.
- **Relics** hum louder the higher you climb. They are pieces of the Tower's older floors, trying to get home.
- **Visitors** on the lower floors: creatures of the high realms come down to see where climbs begin. Even monsters are tourists somewhere.

## Voice Guide (for future writing)

Short declaratives. Concrete nouns. The Tower is spoken of like weather — vast, personal, neither
good nor evil. Humor lives in the small people (urchins, tavernkeeps, pedlars), never in the
Tower itself. Never explain the lid under Aethon. Never name the first climber.
